Moya (Moyang) Li is a scholar who thinks at the intersection of literary form, aesthetics, mathematics, and computing, and an Assistant Professor in English at California State University, Long Beach. Her articles are published or forthcoming in Textual Practice, LIT: Literature, Interpretation, Theory, ASAP, and Journal of Postcolonial Writing. She received her B.A. in Mathematics and Comparative Literature from Columbia University, her Ph.D. in Literatures in English from Rutgers University, and was a fellow at NYU’s Courant Institute for Mathematical Sciences.
